The Consultation

A conversation before a tape. We discuss occasion, wardrobe, the way you move in a room. Cloth is laid out; nothing is hurried.

90 min · in studio

The Measuring

Thirty-two measurements taken by hand, a posture study, notes on how your shoulder falls. No two blocks are alike.

45 min · in studio

The Fittings

Two — sometimes three — baste and forward fittings. The garment is built around you, not the other way around.

4–6 weeks · by appt.

The Finishing

Hand-sewn buttonholes, pick-stitched lapels, a lining cut from silk you chose. Delivered in a garment bag that will outlast most jackets.

The Man & The Brand

A tailor,
and the men
he dresses.

"I learned to make a suit before I learned to buy one. That order mattered. It still does."

— KyrisFounder, Cutter, Last Word

Kyris apprenticed on Savile Row for four years before returning home to open a room of his own. What he brought back was not a brand, but a method — the quiet insistence that a garment should be argued over in French chalk long before it is photographed.

The studio was opened in 2020 with three commissions and a single cutting table. It has, by design, grown slowly. Fewer than sixty bespoke garments are cut in any calendar year. None are made twice.

The rule of the house is simple: if it cannot be done by hand in this room, it is not done at all.
